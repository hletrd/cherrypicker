## Priority 2: Stricter PDF amount pattern (F5)
- **File:** `packages/parser/src/pdf/table-parser.ts` line 5
- **File:** `apps/web/src/lib/parser/pdf.ts` line 22
- Change from `/[\d,]+원?/` to `/[\d,]{3,}원?/` — requires at least 3 digits or comma-separated numbers
- This prevents card numbers like "1234" (4 digits, no comma) from matching as amounts
- Wait — card numbers are 4 digits. Amounts are typically 4+ digits too. Need a better approach:
  - Better: require either 원 suffix, or a comma separator, or 4+ digits (amounts are always >= 1000 won in practice for credit card transactions)
  - Actually `/[\d,]+원?/` is used for ROW DETECTION, not amount parsing. The issue is that it matches too broadly. For row detection, we want rows that have amounts. Credit card amounts are always >= 0 won. But many things have numbers.
  - Best fix: change to `/[\d,]{2,}원?/` but also check that the matched value, when parsed, is >= 100 (to exclude small numbers). Actually this is complex.
  - Simpler fix: just add a word boundary or require the number to not be part of a longer alphanumeric sequence: `/(?<![a-zA-Z-])[\d,]+원?(?![a-zA-Z\d-])/` — this prevents matching within card numbers like "1234-5678".
  - Actually the simplest robust fix: `filterTransactionRows` already requires BOTH a date AND an amount. The issue is if a line has a real date AND a false-positive amount from a card number. But the date pattern requires YYYY.MM.DD format which is unlikely to appear near a card number. The risk is low.
  - Better approach: change `AMOUNT_PATTERN` to `/(?<!\d[\d-])[\d,]+원?/` to avoid matching within hyphenated number sequences (card numbers, phone numbers).
  - Simplest: just require the number to be preceded by space or start-of-string, and optionally followed by space or end: `/(?:^|\s)[\d,]+원?(?:\s|$)/`
